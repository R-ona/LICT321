Digital Libraries (DL) &
Institutional Repositories (IR)

Digital libraries are designed to provide access to a wide range of digital content, including books, journals, multimedia, and other resources. Institutional repositories are designed to collect, preserve, and disseminate the scholarly output of an institution, such as research papers, theses, and dissertations.

In addition, DL focuses on organizing, preserving, and providing access to digital collections while IRs emphasize management of intellectual property; often follow specific standards to ensure interoperability.

"Digital Libraries and Institutional Repositories addresses new methods, practices, concepts, and techniques, as well as contemporary challenges and issues for libraries and university repositories that can be accessed electronically."

- Information Resources Management Association (IRMA), 2020

Collection Management in Libraries: Digitization initiatives, Electronic Resource Management (ERM), and Collaborative efforts

Strategies: Training & Support, User-friendly Interface, Ethical considerations, Providing standards, and Regular evaluation
